## Overview
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

This document presents the main insights from analyzing customer rental behavior, spending patterns, genre preferences, and geographic trends in the Sakila database.

The purpose of the analysis is to convert raw transactional data into clear business insights that support customer retention, revenue growth, and more targeted marketing decisions.

---

## Customer Behavior Insights
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 1. High-Value Customers Drive Revenue
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

The analysis shows that customer value is concentrated in a relatively small segment of the customer base.

- The top customers represent only 20% of all customers
- This group contributes a disproportionately large share of total rentals and revenue
- Customer value is not evenly distributed across the database

**Insight:**  
The business relies heavily on a smaller group of high-value customers. Retaining these customers should be a priority because losing even a few of them would have a noticeable impact on revenue.

---

### 2. Active Customers Contribute Most of the Engagement
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Customer activity status is strongly linked to business performance.

- Active customers account for the majority of the customer base
- They contribute more rentals and more revenue than inactive customers
- Inactive customers represent a much smaller and less engaged segment

**Insight:**  
Customer activity is directly tied to business value. The company should maintain strong engagement with active customers while building targeted reactivation campaigns for inactive customers.

---

## Genre Preference Insights
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 3. A Few Genres Dominate Rental Demand
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Genre demand is concentrated in a smaller number of high-performing categories.

- A few genres consistently generate the highest rental activity
- These same genres also contribute strongly to total revenue
- Lower-performing genres attract less customer interest

**Insight:**  
The business should prioritize high-performing genres in promotions, recommendations, and inventory planning because these categories drive the strongest customer response.

---

### 4. Customers Show Clear Repeat Genre Behavior
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Customer rental behavior shows that many customers do not rent randomly across all genres.

- Some customers repeatedly rent films from the same genre
- Other customers show broader interest across multiple genres
- Favorite genre patterns help explain customer taste and behavior

**Insight:**  
Genre preference is useful for personalization. Customers with strong repeat patterns should receive targeted recommendations based on their most-rented genres.

---

## Revenue Insights
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 5. Revenue Is Concentrated in Specific Segments
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Revenue concentration appears at both the customer level and the genre level.

- High-value customers generate a large share of total revenue
- Top-performing genres contribute a major share of earnings
- Revenue is driven more by strong segments than by equal performance across all groups

**Insight:**  
The business should focus more resources on the customer and genre segments that already generate the strongest returns.

---

### 6. Customer Activity and Spending Move Together
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

The analysis shows a clear link between rental activity and total spending.

- Customers who rent more frequently also tend to spend more overall
- Higher engagement is associated with higher customer value
- Frequent rental behavior is a strong indicator of revenue contribution

**Insight:**  
Strategies that increase repeat rentals are likely to increase overall revenue. Engagement should be treated as a revenue driver, not only a usage metric.

---

## Time-Based Insights
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 7. Rental Activity Changes Over Time
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Rental demand is not constant across the time period analyzed.

- Some months show stronger customer activity than others
- Rental volume fluctuates over time
- Customer participation varies by period

**Insight:**  
The business should use time-based patterns to schedule promotions, campaigns, and engagement offers more effectively.

---

## City-Level Insights
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 8. Rental and Revenue Performance Differ by City
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Customer demand is not evenly distributed across all locations.

- Some cities contribute more rentals than others
- Some cities generate stronger revenue performance
- Geographic differences affect business performance

**Insight:**  
High-performing cities should receive more marketing attention and operational focus, while lower-performing cities may need targeted promotional support.

---

### 9. Genre Preference Is Not the Same in Every City
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Genre popularity changes across locations.

- Certain genres perform better in specific cities
- Customer preferences differ by location
- City-level demand patterns are not uniform

**Insight:**  
Marketing campaigns should be adjusted by city. A single promotion strategy is less effective when customer preferences vary across locations.

---

## Overall Conclusion
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

The analysis highlights four major patterns:

- A relatively small customer segment drives a large share of revenue
- Active customers generate most of the business value
- Genre preference strongly shapes rental behavior
- City-level differences affect both demand and content preference

These findings show that customer behavior, content performance, and geography all play an important role in business success.

---

## Recommendations
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

### 1. Protect the High-Value Customer Segment
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Because the top customers represent only 20% of the customer base but contribute a large share of business value, they should be treated as a priority segment.

Recommended actions:
- Create loyalty rewards for frequent renters
- Offer targeted discounts to top customers
- Use personalized outreach to maintain engagement

---

### 2. Build Reactivation Campaigns for Inactive Customers
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Since inactive customers contribute less engagement and revenue, reactivation efforts should focus on bringing them back into the rental cycle.

Recommended actions:
- Send win-back offers
- Promote popular genres to inactive customers
- Use past rental history to personalize messaging

---

### 3. Promote High-Performing Genres More Aggressively
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Because only a few genres dominate rentals and revenue, marketing resources should be concentrated on those genres.

Recommended actions:
- Feature top genres in campaigns
- Maintain stronger inventory in high-demand genres
- Use popular genres as the main driver of promotions

---

### 4. Personalize Recommendations by Genre Preference
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Since many customers repeatedly rent from the same genre, recommendations should reflect individual taste patterns.

Recommended actions:
- Recommend films from each customer's favorite genre
- Group customers by genre loyalty
- Use genre-based targeting in email and app promotions

---

### 5. Increase Repeat Rental Activity
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Because spending rises with customer activity, the business should encourage customers to rent more often.

Recommended actions:
- Offer repeat-renter incentives
- Introduce bundles or rental streak rewards
- Use timed promotions to encourage more rentals

---

### 6. Plan Promotions Around Demand Timing
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Since rental activity changes over time, promotions should be aligned with stronger and weaker periods.

Recommended actions:
- Increase campaigns during slower periods
- Use high-demand periods for premium promotions
- Track monthly changes to improve timing

---

### 7. Adjust Marketing by City
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Because city performance differs, the business should use location-based strategy instead of treating all cities the same.

Recommended actions:
- Focus resources on high-performing cities
- Support lower-performing cities with targeted campaigns
- Review city-level performance regularly

---

### 8. Tailor Genre Campaigns by Location
[Back to Top](#project-findings-customer-behavior--genre-analysis)

Because genre preference differs across cities, genre-based marketing should be localized.

Recommended actions:
- Promote the top genre in each city
- Customize campaigns using city-level demand patterns
- Avoid applying the same genre promotion everywhere

---
